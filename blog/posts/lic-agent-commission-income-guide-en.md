# How Much Commission Does a LIC Agent Get? (Complete Income Guide)

Becoming an insurance advisor with **:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} (LIC)** is one of the most trusted and stable career options in India.

One of the most common questions aspirants ask is:

> **How much commission does a LIC agent actually earn?**

This guide explains **LIC agent commission clearly, practically, and realistically**.

---

## LIC Agent Commission Structure – Overview

LIC agents earn commission as a **percentage of the premium paid by customers**.  
Commission depends on:

- Type of insurance policy  
- Premium payment mode  
- Policy year (first year or renewal year)

---

## First-Year Commission

In the first year of a policy, LIC agents earn:

- **25% to 35%** of the first-year premium  
- Higher commission on long-term regular premium plans  

**Example:**  
Annual premium = ₹20,000  
→ First-year commission = **₹5,000 to ₹7,000**

---

## Renewal Commission (Long-Term Income)

From the **second year onwards**, LIC pays renewal commission:

- **5% to 7.5% every year**  
- Paid as long as the policy remains active  

**Example:**  
₹20,000 × 6% = **₹1,200 per year** (for many years)

This creates **stable, recurring income**.

---

## Policy-Wise LIC Commission Table

| Policy Type | First Year | Renewal |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| Endowment / Money Back | ~28% | ~5% |
| Whole Life Plans | ~28% | ~5% |
| Term Insurance | ~25% | Very Low / Nil |
| Pension / Annuity | ~7.5% | Nil |
| Single Premium Plans | ~2% | Nil |
